The Master

A Life Devoted to the Sacred Art of Yoga

Born in the sacred foothills of the Himalayas in 1958, Master Arjun Dev Shastri began his yoga journey at the tender age of six under the tutelage of the legendary sage Swami Prakashananda. His childhood was woven with morning rituals at the Ganges, chanting Sanskrit shlokas before dawn, and mastering postures that most adults could never dream of attaining.

By age twenty-two, he had received the highest honor in classical Hatha Yoga — the Yogacharya distinction — from the ancient Gurukula of Rishikesh. But Master Arjun's path was never meant to stay within the mountains. He carried the torch of Vedic wisdom across continents.

"Yoga is not a practice you add to your life — it is the life itself, breathing through every moment you choose to be present."

Today, his ashram in Rishikesh draws thousands of seekers annually, while his online platform has made ancient teachings accessible to over 4.2 million disciples across the globe.

Core Beliefs

The Four Pillars of True Yoga

Master Arjun's teachings rest upon four unshakeable pillars — a synthesis of the ancient Vedic science and the lived wisdom of five decades of practice.

🌬️

Prana — The Breath of Life

Breath is the bridge between the conscious and unconscious mind. Through advanced pranayama, Master Arjun teaches disciples to harness prana — the universal life-force — for healing, clarity, and spiritual awakening. Every breath is a doorway.

🧘

Dhyana — The Art of Stillness

True meditation is not the absence of thought but the deep recognition of the witness beyond thought. His unique Shanta Dhyana technique has helped thousands overcome anxiety, trauma, and existential restlessness — reaching the ground of pure being.

⚡

Shakti — The Divine Energy

The body is not merely physical — it is a luminous field of energy. Master Arjun's Kundalini practices safely awaken the dormant divine power at the base of the spine, channeling it upward through the chakras toward enlightened consciousness.

🌿

Dharma — Right Living

Yoga extends far beyond the mat. Dharmic living means aligning every action, thought, and choice with universal truth. His workshops on Yamas and Niyamas provide a practical ethical framework for navigating modern life with integrity and grace.

🌙

Karma — The Law of Cause

Understanding karma liberates rather than burdens. Master Arjun teaches the ancient science of karma yoga — acting without attachment to outcomes — as the most direct path to freedom available in everyday modern life.

☀️

Moksha — The Ultimate Liberation

All yoga ultimately points toward moksha — liberation from the cycle of suffering and the realization of the self's infinite nature. This is not a distant goal but a living reality available in each moment of sincere practice and self-inquiry.

The Full Story

From Himalayan Foothills to the World Stage — The Extraordinary Journey of Master Arjun Dev Shastri

There are teachers, and then there are masters. Master Arjun Dev Shastri belongs to that rarest of categories — a human being who has not merely studied yoga but has been utterly transformed by it, and who carries that transformation as a living transmission to every soul he encounters.

His story begins not in a city or a university, but in the ancient village of Devaprayag, where the Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers merge to form the sacred Ganges. It was here, in 1958, that Arjun Dev was born to a family of Vedic scholars who had maintained the sacred fire — the dhuni — for seven generations.

"I did not choose yoga. Yoga chose me. The practice was in the air I breathed, the water I drank, the very soil beneath my feet as a child. It was only later I understood what a gift that was."

The Early Years: A Child Among Sages

At the age of six, young Arjun was taken by his grandfather to meet Swami Prakashananda — a 97-year-old sage who had reportedly not eaten solid food in forty years and who could sit in perfect stillness for eighteen hours at a stretch. The encounter changed everything.

"He looked at me," Master Arjun has said in numerous interviews, "and I felt as though he could see not just the child I was, but every life I had ever lived and would ever live. He said only one word: 'Ready.' And from that day, my training began."

The training was rigorous — far beyond anything encountered in modern yoga studios. Young Arjun rose at 3:30 AM each morning for Brahma Muhurta practice, performed over 500 sun salutations each week, memorized thousands of Sanskrit sutras, and learned the intricate energetic maps of the human body as described in the ancient Tantric scriptures.

The Gurukula Years: Mastering the Classical Arts

At the age of sixteen, after a decade of foundational training with Swami Prakashananda, Arjun Dev enrolled at the prestigious Rishikesh Gurukula of Classical Yoga — one of the last remaining institutions where yoga is taught in its complete, unmodified classical form. Here, for six years, he underwent what he describes as "the most challenging and most beautiful period of my life."

The curriculum covered the complete Ashtanga of Patanjali — not merely the asanas that have become so commercially popular in the West, but all eight limbs in their full depth: the ethical disciplines (Yamas and Niyamas), the physical postures (Asanas), the breath sciences (Pranayama), sensory withdrawal (Pratyahara), concentration (Dharana), meditation (Dhyana), and the ultimate state of samadhi.

  • 01 Mastered over 840 classical asana variations, including many regarded as physically impossible by modern standards
  • 02 Completed the rare Kumbhaka Siddhi — the ability to suspend breath for extended periods through advanced pranayama mastery
  • 03 Studied directly under three of the greatest living masters of the 20th century, including the legendary BKS Iyengar
  • 04 Received the Yogacharya title at age 22 — one of the youngest recipients in the institution's 300-year history
  • 05 Authored his first manuscript in Sanskrit on the energetics of the subtle body, which was later translated into 22 languages

The World Tour: Carrying the Flame Across Continents

In 1987, at the age of 29, Master Arjun Dev made a decision that shocked his peers: he would leave the sanctuary of the Himalayas and take the teachings of classical yoga to the world. Armed with nothing but his practice, a threadbare dhoti, and an unshakeable inner conviction, he boarded a plane for the first time — bound for London.

What followed was nothing short of remarkable. Within three months, he had established a small but intensely devoted following in London's Notting Hill. Within a year, he had been invited to teach at Oxford University's Department of Eastern Religions. Within three years, he was holding sold-out retreats across Europe, and his waiting list for one-on-one sessions stretched over two years.

"In the West, I found people who were deeply hungry — not for food, but for meaning. For silence. For something real beneath all the noise of modern life. Yoga met that hunger in a way that nothing else could."

His European success led to invitations across North America, South America, Australia, Japan, and eventually to 68 countries across six continents. He taught in corporate boardrooms and Tibetan monasteries, in Harvard lecture halls and Bali beach pavilions, in maximum-security prisons in California and royal palaces in the Middle East.

The Ashram: A Living Sanctuary of Wisdom

In 2002, Master Arjun returned to India to establish Ananda Shakti Ashram — a world-renowned residential yoga center perched above the Ganges in Rishikesh. The ashram was designed from its foundation to be a living embodiment of his teachings: every tree planted deliberately, every structure aligned with ancient Vastu principles, every space designed to facilitate the deepest possible inner transformation.

Today, the ashram welcomes over 3,000 residential students annually from across the world. Programs range from one-week immersions to three-year teacher training programs that produce some of the world's most respected yoga instructors. The ashram also runs a free school for 240 underprivileged children from the surrounding villages, and a clinic offering traditional Ayurvedic medicine at no cost to local communities.

The Books: Sacred Wisdom in Modern Language

Beyond his teaching, Master Arjun Dev is one of the most celebrated authors in the field of yoga and consciousness studies. His fourteen books have collectively sold over 28 million copies worldwide and have been translated into 42 languages. His seminal work, "The Yoga of Now: A Complete Science of Inner Awakening," spent six consecutive years on international bestseller lists and is used as a primary text in over 300 universities globally.

Other notable works include "Pranayama: The Science of Sacred Breath," "The Chakra Blueprint: Mapping the Subtle Body," and his most personal work, "Himalayan Silence: Letters to My Teacher," — a deeply moving account of his relationship with Swami Prakashananda that has been called "one of the most beautiful spiritual memoirs of the century."

Recognition and Awards: A World Acknowledges Its Teacher

The awards and recognitions that have come to Master Arjun Dev are too numerous to list in full, but several stand out as particularly significant. In 2015, he received the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage Ambassador Award for his work in preserving and transmitting the classical yoga tradition. In 2019, the Government of India honored him with the Padma Bhushan — the nation's third-highest civilian honor.

He has addressed the United Nations General Assembly twice on the role of contemplative practice in world peace. He has been featured on the covers of Time, National Geographic, and over 400 other publications. He has been named in Forbes' list of the "World's 50 Most Spiritually Influential Living People" twelve times consecutively.

The Inner Life: Behind the Legend

Yet for all his global recognition, those closest to Master Arjun Dev consistently speak of a man of profound simplicity. He rises each morning at 3:30 AM — a habit unbroken for nearly five decades — to practice alone in the darkness before the Ganges. He eats once a day. He answers his own correspondence by hand. He spends three months each year in silent retreat.

"Awards are what the world puts on you," he has said with characteristic directness. "Yoga is what you put into the world — one breath, one student, one moment of genuine silence at a time. That is all I have ever tried to do."

"The greatest yoga posture is not the most difficult one. It is the posture of an open heart — available to life exactly as it is, in this very moment. Everything else is preparation for that."

The Legacy: A Living Tradition for Future Generations

As Master Arjun Dev enters his sixty-sixth year, the question of legacy weighs gently on those who love him. He himself seems unconcerned. "The teachings are not mine," he often says. "They belong to no one and to everyone. They were here before I was born, and they will outlast any name attached to them."

Nevertheless, the structures he has built — the ashram, the teacher training program, the foundation, the digital platform that now reaches millions — ensure that the living transmission of classical yoga will continue to flow into a world that needs it more desperately than ever.

The students he has trained now teach in 78 countries. The books he has written sit on millions of nightstands. The silence he carries within him has touched the depths of souls on every continent. In a world of noise, of speed, of surface and spectacle, Master Arjun Dev Shastri stands as living proof that the ancient way still works — that the human being who commits fully to the inner path can become a light that illuminates far beyond the boundaries of any single life.

And every morning, while the world still sleeps, he rises in darkness, sits beside the sacred river, and breathes. As he has always done. As he will always do.
